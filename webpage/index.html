APPETIZERS

It is December 15th, 1966. The date for Super Bowl I has just been announced and UCLA Freshman Lew Alcindor made his collegiate debut last week, breaking the school record for most points scored in a game.

10 days prior, acclaimed animator and film producer, Walt Disney, had just celebrated his birthday. Now he lay dead, 30 minutes from his home in Los Angeles, in a bed at Saint Joseph Hospital. The following day, his funeral would be held and on December 17th, he would be cremated with his ashes interred at Forest Lawn Memorial Park.

It wasn't a freak accident or a tragic event. In fact, the same exact story was occurring for millions of Americans across the U.S.

Disney had been a heavy smoker since his 20s. As a driver for the Red Cross in france, he first took up smoking out of boredom and the general monotony of the miliary. Soon, they became a necessity for him. He loved the feeling they gave him, the focus and power that came with a Brown Chesterfield in between his restless fingers. Even his daughter said she "couldn't picture him without a cigarette".

The result of his death, then, was of course complications relating to his smoking. His official autopsy attributes his death to circulatory collapse caused by his lung cancer.
